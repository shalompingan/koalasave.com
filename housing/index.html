🌿 5 Tips to Save for Your Down Payment Faster
  • Open a high-yield savings account — Earn 4-5% APY instead of near-zero interest at a traditional bank. That's free money on your home fund.
  • Automate your savings — Set up an automatic transfer on payday. You can't spend what you don't see, and consistency builds momentum.
  • Cut housing costs temporarily — Consider moving in with family or getting a roommate for 12-24 months. The sacrifice is temporary, homeownership is forever.
  • Redirect subscription savings — The average American wastes $21/month on unused subscriptions. Cancel them and redirect every dollar to your down payment.
  • Increase your income — A side hustle, overtime, or freelancing for just $500/month adds $6,000/year to your down payment fund.

❓ First Home FAQ (2026)
How much down payment do I need for a house?
Conventional loans typically require 3-20% down. For an $80,000 target, that represents a 16% down payment on a $500,000 home — a solid range to avoid PMI while keeping monthly payments manageable. FHA loans can go as low as 3.5% down.
How much do I need for a down payment with 2026 interest rates?
With 2026 mortgage rates around 6.5% for a 30-year fixed, your down payment matters more than ever. On a $400,000 home, putting 20% down ($80,000) instead of 5% ($20,000) saves roughly $350-500/month in interest and eliminates PMI — that's over $100,000 in savings over the life of the loan.
How can I save for a down payment faster?
Put your savings in a high-yield account (4-5% APY), automate monthly transfers, reduce rent by moving in with family temporarily, or pick up a side hustle. Every extra dollar brings your dream home closer. Use the "What If" slider above to see the impact.
What is PMI and how does it affect my down payment?
Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is required when you put down less than 20%. It typically costs 0.5-1.5% of the loan amount annually — that's $2,000-6,000/year on a $400,000 loan. Reaching 20% equity removes PMI and saves you thousands each year.
