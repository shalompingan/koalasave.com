📊 How Much Does the Average American Spend on Subscriptions in 2026?

According to CNET's April 2026 survey of 2,500+ U.S. adults, the average American spends $111 per month — that’s $1,332 per year — on subscription services. That’s up 23% from the previous year.

Key findings from the same survey:

  • $21/month ($252/year) is wasted on subscriptions people don’t use
  • 89% of consumers underestimate their true subscription spending
  • Millennials average $276/month — nearly 2.5x the national average
  • Gen Z averages $377/month — the highest of any generation
  • 61% of Americans have a streaming/video subscription (most popular category)

If your total is above these averages, you’re not alone — but you’re also leaving serious money on the table.

Sources: CNET/YouGov Subscription Survey 2026, Delo Digital Media Trends, C+R Research.

How much does the average person spend on subscriptions per month?

No. Unlike Rocket Money or Trim, this calculator never asks for bank credentials, Plaid access, or any financial data. You enter your subscriptions manually — no risk, no privacy concerns.
How much money could I save by canceling unused subscriptions?
The average American wastes $21/month ($252/year) on subscriptions they don’t use, according to CNET 2026. Millennials waste even more at $29/month. Use our cancellation simulator above to see exactly how much you could save by trimming just the services you don’t need.
What is subscription fatigue and why is everyone talking about it?
Subscription fatigue is the growing frustration consumers feel from managing too many recurring payments. In 2026, the average household has 5-6 active subscriptions. With prices rising 17%+ since 2020, 40% of consumers have cut back on streaming services due to financial pressure. This tool helps you see the full picture so you can make informed decisions.
How do I cancel a subscription I no longer want?
Start by logging into the service's website or app and navigating to account settings. Most services have a "Manage Subscription" or "Billing" section. Take a screenshot of the cancellation confirmation for your records. After canceling, check your next billing statement to verify the charge stopped. For an extra layer of protection, consider using a virtual card number that can't be charged after you cancel.
